# Paper Reading Template

Use this template for every full-text paper.

## Research Problem

What problem does the paper solve, and why does the problem matter?

## Core Claim

State the author's main claim in one or two precise sentences.

## Method and Materials

Describe the dataset, corpus, experiment, model, theory, or close-reading material. Explain whether the method can support the claim.

## Argument Structure

Map the paper as: problem -> method -> evidence -> interpretation -> conclusion.

## Key Evidence

Bind every important claim to page numbers, sections, quotes, figures, or tables.

## Figures and Tables

For each important figure/table, include figure id, page, caption, screenshot path, and what role it plays in the argument.

## Limitations

Separate author-stated limitations from limitations inferred by the Agent.

## Relevance to User

Explain how this paper helps the user's project: theory, method, evidence, counterexample, background, or research gap.
